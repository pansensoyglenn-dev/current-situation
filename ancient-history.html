The Plot to Annihilate the Jews

From Amalek to Haman, from Hitler to Khamenei

HISTORY often unfolds in patterns. One of the most striking patterns in both Scripture and world history is the recurring attempt to destroy the Jewish people—and the repeated failure of those who plot their annihilation.

The Ancient Enemy: Amalek

The story begins in the Bible with Amalek, one of Israel’s earliest and most relentless enemies. When Israel came out of Egypt, the Amalekites attacked the weak and weary from behind (Exodus 17:8–16; Deuteronomy 25:17–19). Because of this cruelty, God later commanded King Saul to completely destroy the Amalekite kingdom and its king, Agag (1 Samuel 15:2–3).

The Shadow of Haman

Centuries later, the Book of Esther introduces Haman the Agagite. This connection is vital; Haman carried the royal line of Agag and the ancient hostility of Amalek. Through the courage of Queen Esther, the conspiracy was exposed, and Haman was executed on the very gallows he had built for Mordecai.

Modern Ideologies

The pattern resurfaced in the twentieth century with Adolf Hitler. Despite the systematic murder of six million Jews, Hitler's dream of annihilation failed, and the Nazi state collapsed in 1945. Today, similar rhetoric emerges from leadership in Iran, continuing a cycle of threats that have existed for millennia.
